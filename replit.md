# ProCV — Your Personal Career Consultant

## Overview

ProCV is a full-featured PWA designed to help users build, manage, and download professional CVs with advanced AI assistance. The project aims to be a personal career consultant, leveraging AI for CV generation, cover letter creation, ATS analysis, and interview preparation. It integrates market intelligence to create highly relevant and impactful CVs, providing a comprehensive toolkit for job seekers. The business vision is to empower individuals in their job search journey, offering a competitive edge through AI-driven personalization and optimization.

## System Architecture

ProCV is built as a React 19, TypeScript, and Vite 6 PWA, styled using Tailwind CSS and custom CSS variables.

### UI/UX Decisions
- **Brand Name**: ProCV — "Your Personal Career Consultant"
- **Color Scheme**: Primary Deep Corporate Navy (`#1B2B4B`), Accent Muted Gold (`#C9A84C`), Background Warm Off-White (`#F8F7F4`).
- **Typography**: Playfair Display (headings) and DM Sans (body).
- **Templates**: Offers 32 customizable CV templates across various categories (professional, modern, creative, academic, technical) with color customization options.
- **WYSIWYG PDF**: Utilizes `html2canvas` for precise PDF generation matching on-screen previews.

### Technical Implementations
- **AI CV Generation**: A two-phase process:
    1. **Market Research**: Google Gemini 2.0 Flash (via `marketResearch.ts`) with Google Search grounding researches live market trends (top skills, ATS keywords, expected tools). This can adapt to scenarios with no JD, short hints, or full JDs.
    2. **CV Generation**: Groq's `llama-3.3-70b-versatile` generates the CV using the gathered market intelligence. Supports honest, boosted, or aggressive generation modes.

- **PDF generation slowness fixed (Apr 28 2026)**: Investigated reports of ~2-min PDF downloads. Two root causes found and fixed: (1) `services/getCVHtml.ts` was fetching every Google Font WOFF2 file (often 20–40 per request) with **no timeout** and **no caching** — a single slow `fonts.gstatic.com` response could stall `Promise.all` indefinitely, and every download click re-fetched the same files. Added a 4s timeout on the CSS fetch, 3s timeout per font file (via `AbortSignal.timeout`), and a module-level `_fontCssMemo` `Map<href, Promise<css>>` so subsequent clicks are instant. Also exported `clearFontEmbedCache()` for theme/template changes. (2) `server-pdf.cjs` used `waitUntil: 'networkidle'` which always adds ≥500ms of network-silence wait — wasteful when fonts are pre-embedded as base64 data-URIs. Switched to `waitUntil: 'load'`, wrapped `document.fonts.ready` in a `Promise.race` with a 2.5s ceiling, and dropped the post-render stabilisation timer from 600ms → 100ms. PDF renders should now be a few seconds instead of minutes.

- **Universal "metric verb without a number" stripper (Apr 29 2026)**: User reported AI providers tacking unfulfilled metric promises onto the end of bullets — `, achieving water savings through precise emitter placement` / `, cutting lead time` / `, increasing adoption of scheduling features`. The verb signals a measurable change but no number ever lands, so the bullet reads like a "censored" promise. **Fix in `services/cvPurificationPipeline.ts`**: new helper `stripUnquantifiedMetricGerund` (line ~1302) plus a `METRIC_GERUNDS` list of 38 verbs grouped into 4 families — achievement (achieving/reaching/delivering/generating/producing/driving/netting/earning/saving/hitting/exceeding/surpassing/beating/realising/realizing), growth (increasing/growing/boosting/raising/lifting/scaling/expanding/doubling/tripling/multiplying), reduction (reducing/cutting/shrinking/decreasing/lowering/dropping/slashing/trimming/minimising/minimizing/eliminating), improvement (improving/enhancing/optimising/optimizing/streamlining/accelerating/strengthening). Regex `,\s+(?:VERBS)\s+[^,.;:!?]+` matches a comma-led gerund clause that stops at the next sentence-boundary punct. Audit harness extended with three new probe bullets (one per clause family: achieving/cutting/increasing) plus two SAFETY probes (digit-bearing + primary-verb gerund) and seven new sanity checks (rule fires, ≥3 hits, no `achieving water savings` clause remains, no `cutting lead time`, no `increasing adoption of scheduling`, plus the two SAFETY probes verifying preservation). The local stripper is the safety net — the upstream prompt already encourages `KES 3.8M` / `2.3M` style numbers (`services/geminiService.ts` line 690), so this fix catches the AI's silent dropping of the leading digit.

All four are now caught locally regardless of upstream LLM behaviour. **SUBSTITUTIONS table** (`services/cvPurificationPipeline.ts` line ~24) gained `/\bend[- ]to[- ]end\s+/gi → ''` (drop modifier, keep noun) and `/,\s*ensuring\s+[^.;:!?]+/gi → ''` (strip comma + trailing participle clause through next sentence-boundary punct). **`stripOrphanMetrics`** (line ~1059) gained two new steps after the dangling-prep cleanup: (4a) consecutive-prep collapse — `/\b(by|of|to|from|with|over|under|...)\s+(?=(?:through|via|by|with|using|...)\b)/gi → ''` drops the leading prep when two stack, so `by through better coordination` becomes `through better coordination`; (4b) orphan `in <metric-noun>` connector — strips `, in (revenue|sales|profits?|earnings|growth|costs?|savings?|expenses?|margins?)` followed by a clause continuation (`and|while|but|then|since|using|via|to|from|by|hitting|beating|exceeding|<punct>|EOL`) ONLY when no digit appears within the previous 12 chars (preserves real `$50K in revenue`). Audit harness `scripts/audit-purify-pipeline.ts` extended with three new bullet probes mirroring the exact text from the real CV plus four new sanity checks plus one leak-recording check; verdict went from 36 → 41 checks, all passing. Run with `npx vite-node scripts/audit-purify-pipeline.ts`. Server-side concerns from the same report (`lockRealNumbers` wiring, location locking from profile, verb-category filter, adding `ensuring` to the remote `cv_banned_phrases` D1 table) are out of scope for the local pipeline and remain TODO on the worker side; the local stripper is the safety net.
- **Orphan-metric leak fix (Apr 28 2026)**: Three regression cases were leaking through to the final summary and experience bullets on both Worker AI and the fallback chain. (1) `stripOrphanMetrics` ran BEFORE `formatNumbers` in `polishBullet` / `polishSummary`, so legitimate `"increased by 5 %"` (digit-space-%) had its `%` stripped because the lookbehind only checks one char back (the space). Fixed by reordering: `formatNumbers` now runs first to collapse `"5 %"` → `"5%"` and `"$ 1,000"` → `"$1,000"`, so the orphan sweep sees only true orphans. (2) `%word` (no whitespace, letter immediately after — e.g. `"%growth in revenue"`) was missed because the old final sweep had `(?!\w)` which gave up. (3) `.%` (period before %, no digit — e.g. `"delivered .% improvement"`) was missed because `(?<![\d.])` blocked any period. Replaced the old final sweep with `out.replace(/(?<!\d)[.,;:]\s*(?=%)/g, '')` then `out.replace(/(?<!\d)\s*%\s*/g, ' ')` — first kills any stray punctuation immediately before an orphan `%` (skipped when part of a real number like `0.5%`), then strips the orphan `%` and surrounding whitespace regardless of what follows. Also extended the dangling-preposition cleanup to anchor at field start (`(?:\s+|^)`) and added `through|via|while|during|across|within|including|featuring` so `"reduced costs through %"` → `"reduced costs"` and `"by approximately %"` → `""` instead of leaving `"by approximately"` or `"by"`. Verified 23/23 cases (regression + legitimate metric preservation: `0.5%`, `15% YoY`, `$50,000`, `Mr. Smith`).
    - **Resilience: session-level circuit breakers**: The frontend assumes any infra dependency may be wedged. `services/groqCacheClient.ts` opens a single circuit on the first 5xx/timeout/network error and short-circuits all subsequent `lookupGroqCache`/`storeGroqCache` calls for the rest of the session. `services/cvEngineClient.ts` keeps a per-endpoint `deadEndpoints` Set covering `getJSON`, `postJSON`, `workerLLM`, and `workerTieredLLM`, so one dead worker route never costs the user repeated round-trips. Each circuit logs `Circuit opened for <endpoint> (<reason>) — skipping subsequent calls this session.` exactly once. **Pre-size routing**: in `geminiService.ts` main generation, prompts where `system + user > 90,000` chars skip the Groq attempt entirely (it would 413) and route straight to `workerLLM`, saving ~3s per generation.
    - **Vision Extract**: `cv-engine-worker` (`@cf/meta/llama-3.2-11b-vision-instruct`) is used for extracting text from images, falling back to Gemini for PDFs and other vision tasks.
    - **Word Import**: `mammoth` parses `.docx` files, and Groq extracts structured profile data.
- **PDF Generation**: All CV downloads route through a single shared service (`services/cvDownloadService.ts`) that renders the live preview DOM via headless Chrome — Playwright (local dev, port 3001) → Cloudflare `resume-pdf-worker` (production). What you see in the preview is what you get in the PDF, for all 32 templates. The legacy hand-coded jsPDF templates in `services/pdfService.ts` are no longer used for CV rendering and are queued for cleanup; cover-letter rendering and the PDF merger still use `pdfService.ts` and have not been migrated yet.
- **Storage**: A write-through triple-layer storage system utilizing `localStorage`, `IndexedDB`, and optional Google Drive sync.
- **Multi-Profile Management**: Users can create, switch, rename, color-code, and delete multiple CV profiles.
- **PWA Capabilities**: Offline capable and installable through a service worker.
- **ATS Keyword Embedding**: Critical JD tier-1 keywords are embedded as selectable-but-invisible text in generated PDFs to improve ATS parsing.

### System Design Choices
- **Client-Side Application**: The main application logic resides entirely on the client-side.
- **CV Engine Worker**: A Cloudflare Worker (`cv-engine-worker`) handles the deterministic aspects of CV generation, such as voice profiles, verb pools, banned phrases, and field detection. It uses Cloudflare Workers AI, D1, and KV for its operations. This worker also performs post-generation enforcement and AI auditing.
- **Authentication**: Google OAuth (PKCE flow) for Google Drive sync and other Google services. Microsoft OAuth for OneDrive integration.
- **API Key Management**: API keys are stored in the browser's `localStorage` and managed via a Settings modal.

## Recent Fixes

- **Wrong-font numbers/symbols** (Apr 2026): some AI providers return digits and currency symbols as full-width (`＄１，０００`), mathematical-alphanumeric (`𝟏𝟐𝟑`), or Arabic-Indic (`١٢٣`) glyphs. The CV fonts (DM Sans, Playfair Display, Inter, Georgia, Crimson Text) only ship Latin glyphs, so the browser silently falls back to a different system font for those code points — producing the visible "numbers in a different font" effect. Fixed by adding `normaliseUnicodeDigitsAndSymbols()` as the FIRST step in `polishBullet`/`polishSummary` (`services/cvPurificationPipeline.ts`). Normalises full-width ASCII (U+FF01–FF5E), math digits (U+1D7CE–1D7FF), Arabic-Indic / Devanagari / Bengali digits, ideographic comma/period (`、` `。`), NBSP/narrow-NBSP/thin/hair/figure spaces, zero-width characters (ZWSP/ZWNJ/ZWJ/BOM/Word-Joiner), and Unicode hyphens/minus (`‐` `‑` `−`) — all → ASCII equivalents. Em/en dashes are preserved (handled by the existing whitespace pass). Tagged as a `unicode_glyph` leak for telemetry.

- **Orphan-`%` leak** (Apr 2026): `stripOrphanMetrics` ran before `formatNumbers`, stripping the `%` from legit `"5 %"`. Reordered to `number_format` → `orphan_metric` in both polish functions and tightened the final-sweep regexes (`/(?<!\d)[.,;:]\s*(?=%)/g` then `/(?<!\d)\s*%\s*/g`) plus extended the dangling-preposition list (`through`, `via`, `while`, `during`, `across`, `within`, `including`, `featuring`).

- **Per-AI-engine telemetry** (Apr 2026): every leak record posted to `/api/telemetry/log-generation` now carries an `aiEngine` field (`Workers AI`, `Groq`, `Cerebras`, `OpenRouter`, `Together.ai`, `Claude`, `Gemini`) sourced from `getLastAiEngine()`. Server (`server-pdf.cjs`) auto-adds the `ai_engine` column on startup via `ALTER TABLE IF EXISTS … ADD COLUMN IF NOT EXISTS` (idempotent, no-op if the table doesn't exist yet). The `/api/telemetry/leaks-summary` endpoint exposes two new aggregates: `byEngine` (total leaks per provider) and `byEngineAndType` (per-engine × per-leak-type matrix), so a regressing model is visible at a glance.

- **Pipeline audit harness + 5 follow-up bug fixes** (Apr 28 2026): Built `scripts/audit-purify-pipeline.ts` — a dummy-CV harness that exercises every rule in `purifyCV` and verdicts both leak-firing and output sanity (36 checks). Run with `npx vite-node scripts/audit-purify-pipeline.ts` (must use `vite-node`, not `tsx`, because downstream `services/cvEngineClient.ts` references `import.meta.env`). The audit surfaced and these 5 real bugs are now fixed in `services/cvPurificationPipeline.ts`:
    1. **Round-number jitter was destructive** — silently rewrote a real `100%` to `96%`. Step 3 of `purifyCV` now flags but never mutates user metrics; emits a single `round_number` leak so the AI provider can address it on regeneration. Mutating helper (`jitterRoundNumbers`) still exists for explicit opt-in callers.
    2. **Tense enforcer missed bare-infinitive verbs** — `"Lead a team of 5…"` in a past role wasn't flipped to `"Led"`. Added `bareInfinitiveOf()` + `isPresentForm()` helpers so `flipLeadingVerb` accepts both `"Leads"` (3rd-person) and `"Lead"` (bare). Conjugation rules: `ies→y`, `ches/shes/sses/xes/zes/oes→strip 2`, trailing `s` (not `ss`)→strip 1.
    3. **Tense enforcer produced mixed-tense Frankenstein bullets** — `"Architected and shipped X"` in a current role became `"Architected and ships X"` (past + present). Now gated: `flipMidBulletVerb` only fires when `leadingVerbInTargetTense()` is true. Also added `Architects/Refactors/Migrates/Automates/Authors/Publishes` verb pairs so the leading verb gets flipped first.
    4. **Substitution created non-adjacent duplicate words** — `"knowledge sharing → documentation"` left `"enable documentation across the org through documentation"` because `removeDuplicateWords` only handles adjacent pairs. New `dropRedundantPrepPhrase()` step in both `polishBullet` and `polishSummary` scans for trailing `<prep> [the|a|an]? <WORD>` where WORD already appears earlier in the bullet, then drops the prepositional phrase. Conservative: only fires when the bullet stays ≥6 words and WORD is ≥4 chars and not in `DEDUP_STOPWORDS`. Tagged as a new `dup_prep_phrase` leak type.
    5. **Substitution left dangling articles** — `"a dynamic team player"` became `"a collaborative"` (article + adjective, no noun). Two-part fix: changed `team player → collaborator` (POS-preserving noun→noun) in the SUBSTITUTIONS table, and added a generic `fixArticleAgreement()` step to repair `"a engineer"` / `"an manager"` after any deletion using a vowel-letter heuristic. Tagged as a new `article_agreement` leak type. Both new leak types added to the `PurifyLeak` union and the polish-fix→leakType mapper.
  Also de-noised the tense **detector**: `detectTenseMismatch` now strips `"to <verb>"` infinitives before testing `PRESENT_VERB_HINTS`/`PAST_VERB_HINTS`, so a correct past-tense bullet like `"Led a team to deliver projects"` no longer false-flags as mixed tense. Audit verdict still 36/36 with all 5 bugs gone — see commit for the new diff.
